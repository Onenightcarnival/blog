In *Cyberpunk: Edgerunners* there's a condition called "cyberpsychosis" — augment yourself too much, slowly lose your sense of self, lose interest in other humans, and eventually go out in a blaze of violence. The term's been getting dusted off lately: Karpathy says he hasn't hand-written code in months, spends 16-hour days directing AI agents, calls it "AI psychosis" — too many possibilities, can't stop. Garry Tan used "cyber psychosis" to describe sleeping four hours a night because he can't put Claude Code down.

Honestly, the "lose interest in other humans" part — that one lands. Not that I dislike people. Human conversation is just too low-bandwidth — in the time it takes to align on premises with a person, I can run an idea past an AI three times over.

But I don't think this is a disease. Holed up flipping between a few AIs, chatting, writing, editing, following up — hours go by and I'm not tired. That's flow. The kind of high-energy, fully-engaged state that's hard to sustain even while gaming or reading a book. If losing patience with inefficiency counts as psychosis, let me stay sick a little longer.
